Official Format

Pickleball Tournament Rules

Eight rules govern every match, from the opening round robin serve to the championship's final point.

1Tournament Format
  • All brackets follow a round robin format.
  • Each team plays every other team in its bracket.
  • The top 2 teams from each bracket advance to the Knockout Round.
  • Ranking basis: 1) Total wins · 2) Point differential · 3) Head-to-head result.
2Round Robin Match Format
  • Each match is one game only, played to 11 points.
  • No win-by-2 rule.
  • At 10–10, a single sudden death point decides the winner.
3Knockout Round Format
  • Single elimination. Matches played to 15 points.
  • No win-by-2 rule.
  • At 14–14, a single sudden death point decides the winner.
4Final Round (Championship)
  • The final is played to 15 points, win by 2 required.
  • At 14–14, play continues until a team leads by 2 points — no score limit.
5Default Rule
  • Teams must be ready when called.
  • 5-minute grace period only.
  • Failure to appear results in a default loss.
6Player Conduct
  • Players must observe proper sportsmanship at all times.
  • Misconduct may result in: a warning, a point penalty, or disqualification.
7Officiating
  • An umpire is assigned to every game.
  • Players may call the ball "in" or "out."
  • Final decision on all calls rests with the umpire.
8Final Authority
  • Tournament organizers may adjust rules if necessary to keep play fair and on schedule.
